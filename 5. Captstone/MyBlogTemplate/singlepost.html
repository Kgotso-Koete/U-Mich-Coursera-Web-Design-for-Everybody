Jozi Ghoul — Me and Thembi forever

Vampires Ghouls, Immortals, Blood
20 Comments December 07, 2013 9:00 PM

I remember a dream in which I found myself in a dark allay in what looked like downtown Joburg, just after 10 pm.

I was standing just outside a nightclub in a passage hidden from the main entrance. A gang of “Rosebank” type dope queens walk into the alley to have a smoke. They are dressed in leather jackets, funky hair and contact lenses.

They approach me. I assume they need to borrow a lighter. I notice that one of them is…Thembi Seete, a legendary member of Boom Shaka that has not aged a day since the 1993 release of the Kwaito/Pop hit, ‘Its about time’. They approach me. I assume they need to borrow a lighter. I notice that one of them is…Thembi Seete, a legendary member of Boom Shaka that has not aged a day since the 1993 release of the Kwaito/Pop hit, ‘Its about time’.

They approach me. I assume they need to borrow a lighter. I notice that one of them is…Thembi Seete, a legendary member of Boom Shaka that has not aged a day since the 1993 release of the Kwaito/Pop hit, ‘Its about time’.

They approach me. I assume they need to borrow a lighter. I notice that one of them is…Thembi Seete, a legendary member of Boom Shaka that has not aged a day since the 1993 release of the Kwaito/Pop hit, ‘Its about time’. I have seen Thembi Seete around the Rosebank Starbucks when I was scavenging for free Wi-Fi. She also wore a leather jacket, black leggings and Adidas sneakers and she gave me eye contact then and now too.

The cool girl gang and their legendary Kwaito flame of a leader approach me to ask for a firelighter when they all begin touching me. I was hard and then I was scared when their nails began to pierce my skin.

Within a flash I was —

At the top of a building, it may have been Carfax in Newtown.

Within a blink of an eye —

Thembi Seete pulled herself up onto the roof, fangs all sharp, eyes glowing, curly hair on fleek, not a scratch on her nail polish. Fuck I always suspected that Thembi might be vampire — those eyes were not glowing because of contact lenses after all.

Two black dudes in leather jackets and tight jeans landed on the Carfax roof like that meteor that crashed into Kimberly, or was it the Free State? Also, I did not think vampires wore skinny jeans.

One of them gave me the look that those dudes at News Cafe give when they offer you a shot of Vodka to welcome you into their squad. He said —

“You are one us now”
